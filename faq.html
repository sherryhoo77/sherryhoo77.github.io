What is a crypto airdrop?

A crypto airdrop is when a blockchain project distributes free tokens to eligible wallet addresses. Projects use airdrops to reward early users, grow their community, and decentralize token ownership. In 2026, major airdrops like MetaMask (MASK) and Polymarket (POLY) are distributing tokens worth hundreds to thousands of dollars to qualifying users.

How do I qualify for crypto airdrops in 2026?

To qualify: (1) Set up a non-custodial wallet like MetaMask or Phantom. (2) Use the protocol genuinely — swap, bridge, provide liquidity, or participate in governance. (3) Maintain consistent activity over weeks and months, not just one-time transactions. (4) Use one wallet per protocol to avoid anti-sybil disqualification. (5) Check for snapshot deadlines and ensure your activity is recorded before the cutoff date.

What is anti-sybil detection and how do I avoid it?

Anti-sybil detection identifies wallets that appear to be one person claiming multiple airdrops. To avoid disqualification: use only one wallet per protocol, vary transaction amounts slightly, spread activity over weeks not hours, use protocols for their actual purpose, and never fund multiple activity wallets from one source in an obvious pattern.
